Problems within the Amazon

The Amazon rainforest is being effected every single day by the amount of trees being cut down and during
this process many diffrent animals and native people to that land are being forced to change environments
We having provided many solutions being Donations, Recycling, Re-planting trees and Spreading the word
This threat on the Amazon Rainforest has impacted the species which inhabit the rainforest as well as the
indigenous people whom live on the lands of the rainforest. The rainforest is at a huge risk of many species
becoming extinct and resulting in many of there habitats to be destroyed and lack of food source results in
a overall decrease in there population. But the indigenous people are also getting affected with there food
source and there population will be hit with a major decrease. The indigenous people are very important as
they are an important role when it comes to south americas fruit and vegetables as the indigenous people
create farms in the amazon rainforest for crops such as Bananas, Coconuts and Pineapples just to name a few.

There is a estimate of 400 tribes which live in the Amazon Rainforest with diffrent cultures, Language and
diffrent religions and beliefs. Due to illegal mining done within the Amazon Rainforest it is destroying the
land and environment which there way of maintaining a food source as well as the mecury found in the mines
from the people mining all the gold it is poisoning the indigenous peoples water source. People use fire to clear
out the land for farming and grazing but by doing this they are effecting the animals and there habitats by
destroying them. Over 10,000 species in the Amazon Rainforest are at a risk of extinction due to deforestation
examples being Capybara, Blue Macaw and South American Tapir. Most of these animals are at a risk due to no habitat.
